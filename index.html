The Science

Why Lens Clarity Matters

The eye lens must remain optically clear to focus incoming light onto the retina. Three mechanisms govern this — all estimable through image analysis.

🔬

Protein Order

Lens transparency depends on the short-range ordered arrangement of crystallin proteins that allows coherent light transmission.

⚗️

Aggregation

Aging and oxidative stress cause proteins to clump, disrupting the ordered lattice and increasing local refractive index variation.

💡

Brightness Distribution

In a clear lens under close-range illumination, reflected light concentrates centrally. Scatter redistributes brightness peripherally.

Scientific Foundation

Scientific Evidence

Lensight's analytical approach draws from established biomedical optics research on lens transparency and scattering.

Benedek, G.B. — 1971

Theory of Transparency of the Eye

Investigative Ophthalmology. Established that lens transparency requires short-range ordered crystallin protein arrangement. Protein aggregation disrupts this order, producing observable light scattering increasing with cataract severity.

Datiles et al. — 1991

Light Scattering in the Human Lens

Archives of Ophthalmology. Demonstrated that dynamic light scattering detects early cataractous changes before clinical symptoms appear — validating scattering intensity as a quantifiable early-stage biomarker.

On Radial Intensity Analysis: In a healthy lens illuminated from close range, reflected light concentrates centrally. Protein aggregates scatter photons outward, elevating peripheral brightness relative to central brightness. The Scatter Index (peripheral ÷ central) captures this redistribution as a dimensionless metric — a simplified, non-clinical approximation of clinical retroillumination photography.
